Hello, I'm Diogo!


A little bit about me

My name is Diogo Ribeiro, I was born on the 6th of December, 1992, in Chaves, Portugal, where I lived until the age of 17. After 12th grade, I moved to Lisbon where I studied Informatics in IST. After a year, I decided to move to Porto, and transferred to FEUP, where I continued to study Informatics. I chose this field because I always had an interest in technology, especially in computers.

I love walking around town during the day, chilling in the caf√© with my friends at night, sunbathing in the beach, swimming in the pool and playing videogames.



What moves me


Videogames are one of my main interests. They are one of the reasons my interest in technology developed in me as I grew up. In my young years, I used to disassemble controllers and even consoles just to see how they looked inside, and I used to re-wire some controllers as they broke. I started to read about videogame development and even developed some simple games using basic engines. This also made my interest in programming grow.

Studying Informatics


As I studied new things in the university, new interests also developed in me. I grew an interest in almost every subject I had contact with, being Networks, Databases or Computer Graphics.

My interests


The specific fields I am interested in are Videogame Design and Development, Computer Graphics, Alternate and Virutal Reality and Programming.

Some of my projects



Forbidden Nebula

My current work in progress, a semi-infinite On-Rails shooter for Android.

A marine gets stranded in a dangerous area of the galaxy, the Forbidden Nebula, where the environment is not visitor-friendly, and a meteor could hit your head at any time, the perfect place for the space pirates' hideout! Fight your way through the treacherous environment and try to escape the Forbidden Nebula!

Playable version available and updated regularly here

IdeaTouch

IdeaTouch, a very simple and easy to use ideation/idea management platform, which is result driven. IdeaTouch emulates a multi-touch system using individual devices. Each person may use their smartphone, tablet or computer to access IdeaTouch and contribute with their ideas and opinions on a problem under discussion.

Hit the Beat

A rythm-based videogame developed for Microsoft, in the course of LGP. Developed in the Unity engine, it was developed as a Universal App, running in all Windows devices. Gameplay experience on a smartphone or a tablet is different than in the computer, which also features Xbox One/360 controller support.

Guardians of Tech

A tower defense game, developed in the course of Digital Games, at FEUP. The player must carefuly lay his defenses, using circuits to connect the different towers to the main power unit, and defend the last remnants of a human technology that will allow the rebuilding of Earth after an Alien apocalypse.

Project.EXE

An action platformer videogame, developed in the course of Digital Games at FEUP. The player must control a prototype robot and lead the revolution to help his fellow robots escape slavery. Demo available here.
